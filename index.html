Abstract

Plastic pollution entering into waterways and oceans poses significant threats to ecological systems and human health. Current methods of assessing marine debris are labor-intensive, time-consuming, and heavily reliant on manual image analysis. To address these challenges, we propose an Artificial Intelligence-assisted Floating Litter Detection (AIFLD) system designed to automate debris identification. By integrating cameras with object detection algorithms, our system can accurately detect and classify different types of marine litter. Our approach enables waste managers and policymakers to efficiently identify litter hotspots, supporting the development of more targeted, cost-effective, and sustainable waste management strategies.
